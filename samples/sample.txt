Artificial intelligence is transforming how software is built. 
Developers who understand both the theory and the practical 
implementation of AI systems are becoming increasingly valuable.

Machine learning is a subset of AI that allows systems to learn 
from data without being explicitly programmed. Instead of writing 
rules by hand, you train a model on examples and it learns the 
patterns itself.

There are three main types of machine learning. Supervised learning 
uses labelled data — you show the model inputs and the correct 
outputs, and it learns the mapping. Unsupervised learning finds 
patterns in unlabelled data. Reinforcement learning trains an agent 
to make decisions by rewarding good outcomes and penalising bad ones.

Large language models like GPT and Claude are trained on vast amounts 
of text data. They learn to predict the next token in a sequence, 
and from that simple objective they develop surprisingly capable 
reasoning and language understanding.

RAG — Retrieval Augmented Generation — is a technique that gives 
a language model access to external knowledge at inference time. 
Instead of relying only on what the model learned during training, 
you retrieve relevant documents and include them in the prompt. 
This makes the model more accurate and keeps its knowledge current.

AI agents go one step further. Instead of a single prompt and 
response, an agent can use tools, make decisions, and chain multiple 
steps together to complete a task. This is the direction most 
production AI systems are moving toward.
